Ventilation

Creating condensation is unavoidable, so we need to be able to allow humid air to escape the home to be replaced by dry air from outside.


Modern windows are usually fitted with Trickle Vents. These vents allow small amounts of air to exchange without the need for mechanical extraction.

According to the UK Government website , trickle vents should be permanently open - even in winter.

Extractor Fans are vital in removing humid air, particularly in bathrooms and kitchens. they should be turned on whenever steam is being created, either by cooking or bathing.


If an extractor fan is available in a utility room; it should be used while a washing machine or tumble drier is operating.

The UK Government website recommends they should be cleaned regularly.

A useful tip to determine whether a fan is not working; is to hold a piece of tissue paper in front of it, while it is switched on. If the paper doesn't stick to the fan, then it is not working.
